**[[Almariel, Lady of Light]]** stood upon [[Eldara, the Shimmering Veil]], her heart aching as she surveyed the creations born from [[Vorthar, The Dark Weaver]]’s corruption. She had once believed in his potential for light, a vision of a future where the Ethyri’s work would be a harmonious blend of darkness and light. But Vorthar’s unchecked ambition had defiled this vision, leaving only shadows and bitterness.

She summoned Vorthar to the heart of Eldara. When he arrived, his form was wreathed in shadows, the once vibrant colors of his presence dulled to pitch-black voids. As they faced each other, Almariel’s radiant light dimmed, and for the first time, a dark fire glowed in her eyes.

“Why, Vorthar?” she asked, her voice steady but tinged with sorrow. “Why twist the hearts of our children? We created them to bring life, not to fashion beings to sate your malcontent.”

Vorthar’s gaze was unrepentant. “They are mine as much as yours, Almariel,” he replied coldly. “And life cannot be made of light alone. Our children needed to know the true nature of balance, and I offered them that power.”

Seeing no way to reconcile, Almariel’s resolve hardened. “Then we are no longer fit to guide them. If you cannot abandon this path, then neither of us should be allowed to meddle further.” Almariel, with sorrow deepening her voice, extended her hand toward Vorthar, who stood in the shifting shadows of Eldara’s heart. “We cannot continue this way, my love,” she said quietly, yet firmly. “Our influence, mingling with creation itself, can no longer remain unchecked. If we leave this world to our children, then it must be theirs to shape without our constant interference. Only then can they truly discover who they are.”

Vorthar clenched his jaw, his usual defiance wavering as he searched Almariel’s face. He saw the grief that mirrored his own—both for the universe they had so lovingly shaped and for the fractured bond between them. In that moment, his resentment softened, if only slightly, as he too understood the gravity of the choice before them. Their love for their children, twisted as it had become, was still a shared burden.

After a long silence, Vorthar gave a reluctant nod. “So be it,” he said, his voice bitter but resigned. “But know this, Almariel, I relinquish my influence only because it is your wish.” His words held a trace of defiance, but a part of him knew that to let go was the only way forward, even if it left him bereft of the control he so craved.

Together, they clasped hands, and a powerful invocation of separation took shape. As they spoke in unison, their light and shadow intertwined one final time before severing into two distinct energies that slowly withdrew from Eldara. The essence of their influence dissipated from the Material Plane, leaving behind only faint traces within their children and the beings they had created. As the spell completed, they released each other’s hands. Almariel’s gaze softened, while Vorthar’s face remained stern, his pride preventing him from showing any trace of the sorrow he felt. With their fates now diverged, they turned away from each other, knowing that the bond they once shared would forever be tainted by the choices they had made.

In the silence that followed, a heavy sense of finality filled Eldara. The Aevari, now free from their parents' constant guidance, were left to shape the world with their own hands, bearing the full responsibility of creation—a gift, and a burden, that they would come to understand in time.

With the Ethyri’s departure, the **Aevari** stood alone in Eldara, free to shape the world according to their will. Together, the four of them, **Feron, Pyrael, Galeon,** and **Valora** took on the task of shaping the physical world to best sustain their creations.

- **[[Feron, Aevari of the Earth]]** took to the mountains and plains, raising vast ranges and carving deep caverns. His mighty hand sculpted the land, fashioning fertile valleys and solid ground where creatures of all kinds could thrive. He brought forth **forests**, **caves**, and **mineral-rich veins** beneath the earth, bestowing resources that would sustain life and ensure survival.

- **[[Pyrael, Aevari of Fire]]** embraced the sky and the realm of fire, his creative vision touching everything from the flickering **flames within the earth’s core** to the dancing **auroras in the heavens**. He crafted volcanoes as conduits for his essence, and from these, the land erupted with fertile soil. Pyrael’s touch invigorated the earth, ensuring that every cycle of destruction would bring new growth and renewal.

- **[[Galeon, Aevari of Air]]**, with his power over wind and air, fashioned the skies and the ever-shifting weather patterns. With a sweep of his hand, **clouds, storms, and gentle breezes** arose, filling the sky with a dynamic energy that would nourish the earth. He created pathways of wind and rivers in the air, channels that would carry seeds and life across Eldara, ensuring that all beings could feel the freedom of movement and change.

- **[[Valora, Aevari of Water]]** delved into the depths of the seas, rivers, and lakes, her power filling the waters with life and mystery. She called forth the tides and currents, creating hidden reefs and caverns beneath the waves where her creatures could thrive. Oceans roared with the life and bounty she instilled, and rivers carved paths across the land, connecting distant lands and sustaining every living thing that would call Eldara home.

Through the Aevari’s efforts, Eldara took shape as a harmonious and beautiful world, a balance of elements and ecosystems that reflected their care and creativity. But beneath this beauty, the seeds of discord sown by Vorthar continued to grow. Though he could no longer touch the Material Plane directly, his dark influence lingered in the hearts of his children, a constant reminder of the power of envy and ambition.

Thus, the Aevari, though bound by their sense of duty and love for their creations, found themselves grappling with an unsettling truth: they were now alone, bearing the burden of creation in a world where both light and shadow would forever vie for dominion.
