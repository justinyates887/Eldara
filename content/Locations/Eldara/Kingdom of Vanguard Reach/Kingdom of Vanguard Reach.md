## Government Structure

**What is the basic form of government in this country?**

Socialist constitutional monarchy. The King handles military affairs, foreign policy, and ensures equitable distribution of resources, while an elected Parliament manages domestic policy and economic coordination. Worker cooperatives form the economic foundation, with democratic councils making production decisions.

**What services does the government or head-of-state provide?**

- Universal military training and defense (mandatory service)
- Coordination of worker cooperatives and resource distribution
- State-run magical colleges focused on military applications
- Healthcare through cooperative medical guilds
- Education including mandatory combat training
- Forge production coordination (weapons, armor, tools distributed based on need)
- Infrastructure maintenance (roads, bridges, fortifications)
- Veteran support and disability services
- Diplomatic relations with other kingdoms
- Emergency disaster response and relief
- Dispute resolution between cooperatives
- Quality standards enforcement for forge products

**Are schools, wells, courts, and the army paid for by taxes?**

Yes, all funded through progressive taxation. Schools provide mandatory education including military training. Wells and public infrastructure are maintained by the state. Courts operate at local and national levels, all tax-funded. The army, as the cornerstone of Vanguard Reach culture, receives substantial funding and is entirely tax-supported. Vanguard Reach centralizes funding through national taxation to ensure equal access across all regions.

**What local or private services are provided by the government?**

Local elected councils handle immediate community needs: maintenance of local infrastructure, coordination with nearby cooperatives, dispute mediation within communities, and local court proceedings. However, even these "local" services are funded through the centralized tax system to ensure equal quality regardless of a community's wealth. There are few truly "private" services - most are either government-run or worker cooperatives. Private enterprise exists only in small-scale personal services (individual artisans, small shops) that don't compete with cooperative

**What services do people expect from their government?**

Citizens expect comprehensive support from cradle to grave. This includes guaranteed military training, access to cooperative employment, fair distribution of resources regardless of individual wealth, protection from external threats, veteran care, and arbitration when cooperative disputes arise. The government is expected to actively prevent wealth concentration and ensure no citizen lacks necessities. Vanguard Reach citizens view active governance as essential to maintaining their egalitarian society. Military readiness and collective security are considered fundamental government responsibilities.

**What do people owe their government?**

Citizens owe mandatory military service (typically 2-4 years starting at age 18), progressive taxation based on income and cooperative profits, participation in local council elections and civic duties, and adherence to cooperative principles. All able-bodied citizens must complete combat training and remain in the reserves. Workers in cooperatives contribute their labor to collective production. The cultural expectation is that everyone contributes according to their ability for the collective good.

**Do people pay their government in taxes, in labour, in crops, in military service?**

All forms. Progressive taxation on cooperative profits and individual income is the primary method. Military service is mandatory for all citizens. Labor contributions through cooperative work are expected, with production coordinated by worker councils. Cooperatives may fulfill tax obligations through provision of goods (weapons, armor, food) directly to state distribution networks when needed. The system is designed to accept contribution in whatever form serves the collective best.

**Who has the right to levy taxes?**

Parliament has the authority to set tax rates and structure through democratic vote. The King can propose tax adjustments related to military needs or resource distribution, but Parliament must approve. Local councils cannot levy additional taxes beyond the national system - all taxation is centralized to ensure equitable burden across the kingdom. Emergency war taxes can be implemented by joint decree of King and Parliament. The system prevents regional inequality by maintaining uniform tax policy.

**For what purposes are taxes (or new taxes) levied?**

Taxes fund the comprehensive socialist system: military maintenance and training, state-run magical colleges, healthcare cooperatives, education, infrastructure, forge production coordination, veteran support, and resource distribution networks. New taxes are levied for major military buildups, expansion of cooperative industries, large infrastructure projects (fortifications, roads, public buildings), disaster recovery, or when resource distribution requires additional funding. All tax increases require Parliamentary approval with public justification of need.

**On what or on whom are taxes levied?**

Taxes are levied on cooperative profits (shared among all members), individual income from non-cooperative work, property ownership (particularly larger holdings), and goods sold outside cooperative distribution networks. All citizens and cooperatives pay proportionally based on their economic activity. The progressive system ensures those with greater means contribute more. Foreign merchants trading within Vanguard Reach pay import/export duties.

**Can taxes be paid in-kind, or do certain things always require money?**

Taxes can be paid in-kind, particularly by cooperatives. Forge cooperatives may provide weapons and armor directly to the military. Agricultural cooperatives can supply food to state distribution. However, administrative costs and foreign trade require currency. Individual citizens typically pay in coin since their economic activity doesn't produce bulk goods. The flexibility allows cooperatives to contribute their actual production rather than converting everything to currency.

**Who provides support services for the head of state and what are they called (examples: councilors, ministers, secretaries, viziers)?**

The King is advised by:

- **The War Council**: Senior military commanders who advise on defense and strategic matters
- **The Forgemaster General**: Coordinates all forge cooperatives and weapon production
- **The High Magistrate**: Oversees the judicial system
- **The Chancellor of Distribution**: Ensures equitable resource allocation across the kingdom
- **The Marshal of Training**: Manages mandatory military service programs
- **The Diplomatic Envoy**: Handles foreign relations (reports to King)

These positions are appointed by the King with Parliament approval, ensuring both executive authority and democratic oversight.

**Are offices hereditary, elected, or appointed?**

The monarchy is hereditary. All other national offices are appointed by the King with Parliamentary approval. Parliament members are elected by regional districts through democratic vote. Local council members are directly elected by their communities. Worker cooperative leadership positions are elected democratically by cooperative members. No positions except the monarchy pass through bloodline. This ensures merit based leadership while maintaining the stability of hereditary rule.

**Can a government office be a career choice?**

Yes, particularly in administration, military command, and cooperative coordination roles. Career civil servants manage the bureaucracy that coordinates the socialist economy. Military officers can serve their entire lives. Magistrates and judges build careers in the legal system. Forge coordinators and distribution managers are professional positions. However, all must have completed mandatory military service first. The comprehensive government means many career paths exist in public service.

**Is the relative power of a country or ruler usually measured by the size of the army, the number and ability of the wizards, or the amount of money and trade flowing through it?**

The size, training, and equipment quality of the army. Vanguard Reach's power stems from having the most formidable military force in Eldara. Every citizen is combat-trained, forge production is optimized for military needs, and the Spartan culture values martial excellence above wealth. The state magical colleges exist primarily to support military applications. Economic strength matters only insofar as it sustains military readiness. A strong army is both power and cultural identity.

**Who will take over running the government if the current head-of-state is incapacitated?**

The designated heir (typically eldest child) assumes command if old enough and capable. If the heir is too young or the succession unclear, the War Council governs military matters while Parliament handles domestic policy until succession is resolved. The Queen or Prince Consort has no constitutional authority to rule alone. In extreme crisis, Parliament can appoint a Regent from the royal family. The system prioritizes clear chain of command given the military focus.

**How is succession determined?**

Hereditary through the royal bloodline. The eldest child inherits regardless of gender. The Spartan culture values martial competence, so heirs undergo the same mandatory military service and additional leadership training. If an heir proves fundamentally unfit (cowardice, incompetence, treason), Parliament can petition to skip them in favor of the next in line, but this requires supermajority vote and is extremely rare. The system balances tradition with practical military necessity.

**Is there an heir apparent (either actual or political)?**

Yes, heirs are publicly known and groomed from childhood. They receive enhanced military training, study strategy and governance, shadow the King in decision-making, and often command units during their mandatory service. The heir apparent typically holds a ceremonial military rank and sits on the War Council as an observer. Public awareness of succession prevents the power struggles that plagued the First Era.

**What happens if the heir is a child?**

A Regent is appointed by Parliament from senior royal family members, typically someone with proven military and administrative experience. The War Council maintains military command while the Regent handles other royal duties. The child heir continues their education and training but makes no binding decisions. Regency ends when the heir reaches age 21 or proves competence through military achievement. The system has clear protocols to prevent power vacuums.

**Who is responsible for protecting the head-of-state?**

The **King's Guard**, an elite unit of 50-100 highly trained warriors sworn to protect the monarch and royal family. Selection is merit-based through competitive trials, not bloodline. Service is voluntary but extremely prestigious. They combine bodyguard duties with ceremonial functions. Additionally, the King's personal combat training (mandatory for all royals) means they can defend themselves if needed, reflecting the ideal that leaders fight alongside their people.

**What safeguards does the head-of-state have against assassins, poison, assault, and magical attack?**

The King's Guard provides physical protection. All food is prepared by trusted cooperative kitchen staff with rotating duties to prevent conspiracy. Magical protection is minimal due to restrictions in place from [[The Pact of The Ythraewyn]] - permanent wards are considered wasteful. Instead, the state magical colleges maintain contracts with certified mages who can provide emergency protective magic if credible magical threats are identified, but this requires justification to the [[Tribunal of Balance]]. The King, like all citizens, receives combat training and is expected to defend themselves physically. Most security relies on the King's Guard and the difficulty of organizing conspiracy in a transparent cooperative society where loyalty is cultural rather than purchased.

**Who can give orders (to the military, to the tax collectors, to the civil servants, to ordinary folks on the street)?**

The King commands the military and can issue directives on resource distribution. Parliament commands civil servants, tax collectors, and domestic administration. Local councils give orders within their jurisdictions. Cooperative councils direct their own workers. Military officers command their units. No one can give arbitrary orders to ordinary citizens - even the King must work through established chains of command. The system balances centralized military authority with democratic civilian governance.

**How are the people with the power to give orders chosen?**

The King inherits authority. Parliament members are elected by popular vote. Military officers are promoted based on merit, combat performance, and leadership ability demonstrated during service. Cooperative leaders are elected by their workers. Local council members are elected by their communities. Civil servants are hired through competitive examination. Magistrates are appointed by the King with Parliament approval. All positions require prior military service.

**Are any activities licensed or certified (driving, dog ownership, being an attorney or a physician)?**

Military service certification is universal - all citizens receive documentation of their training completion. Forge work requires cooperative membership approved by worker councils based on skill demonstration. Physicians and healers are certified through medical cooperatives after apprenticeship. Attorneys and magistrates must pass examinations administered by the High Magistrate's office. Magical practitioners require certification under the Pact of the Ythraewyn, handled through the state magical colleges. Master craftspeople earn titles through cooperative recognition. Most licensing focuses on skills critical to collective welfare or Pact compliance.

**Who does the certification or licensing?**

Cooperatives certify their own members through democratic worker councils. The High Magistrate's office certifies legal professionals. Medical cooperatives certify healers. The state magical colleges handle all magical certification and Pact compliance. Military certification is automatic upon service completion. The Chancellor of Distribution's office licenses merchants conducting inter-kingdom trade. Local councils may license small-scale personal services. The system distributes certification authority to relevant expert bodies rather than centralizing it.

**Is it merely formal (pay a fee, and get a license), or are there qualifications to meet?**

Qualifications are rigorous. Cooperative membership requires demonstrated skill and worker council approval - no one buys their way in. Legal professionals must pass comprehensive examinations. Medical training involves years of apprenticeship. Magical certification requires meeting Pact standards and proving competence. The culture values merit and collective benefit over wealth, so purchasing licenses is antithetical to the system. Even foreign merchants must demonstrate honest dealing before receiving trade licenses.

**Can licenses or certifications be revoked, and if so, how?**

Yes. Cooperatives can expel members through democratic vote for incompetence, dangerous behavior, or violation of cooperative principles. The High Magistrate can disbar legal professionals for corruption or malpractice. Medical cooperatives revoke certification for negligence. Magical licenses are revoked by enforcement agencies under Pact violations. Military dishonor can result in service record amendment. Revocation requires documented cause and often involves hearings where the accused can defend themselves. Appeals go to Parliament for non-magical matters, the Tribunal for magical ones.

## National Economy

**What goods are produced in this country and where are they produced?**

Weapons, armor, and steel tools are produced in forge cooperatives concentrated around [[★ Ithilvaeth]] and other major cities. Quarries in the mountainous western regions extract limestone and granite for construction. Agricultural cooperatives in river valleys and cleared lands produce grains, vegetables, and raise livestock, though not enough to be fully self-sufficient. State magical colleges train battle-mages who then work through cooperative contracts. Smaller cooperatives produce everyday goods: textiles, pottery, leather goods, woodwork. Most production is cooperative-based with facilities in urban centers where workers can efficiently collaborate.

**What are the major trade goods?**

Weapons, armor, and tools are the primary exports - the finest human-made steel in Eldara at prices below dwarven makes. Quarried stone for roads and construction. Mercenary warriors and battle-mages hired for protection contracts. Magical enforcement and investigation services under Pact contracts. Military advisors and fortification engineers. Quality steel tools for farming and construction. The kingdom's reputation rests on martial excellence translated into trade goods.

**Are there trading centres?**

Ithilvaeth serves as the primary trading hub where foreign merchants meet cooperative representatives. Major forge districts exist to  have designated markets for bulk transactions. Border towns with Lenthir ([[Brookshade]]) facilitate grain imports. Coastal settlements ([[Ghraedhmar]]) trade with maritime kingdoms. Each trading center has cooperative councils that negotiate terms collectively rather than individual merchants competing. The system ensures fair prices and prevents exploitation while maintaining strong trade relationships.

**What economic systems are used?**

Mixed system centered on cooperative principles. Within cooperatives, members receive shares of profits through democratic vote rather than wages. External trade uses gold standard currency for foreign transactions. Letters of credit from established cooperatives are accepted for large purchases. Stock companies don't exist . The concept of private ownership and shareholding contradicts the cooperative model. Barter occurs in small-scale personal exchanges but isn't systemic. The economy prioritizes collective benefit and equitable distribution over individual wealth accumulation.

**Are there banks?**

Yes, but structured as credit cooperatives rather than private banks. These cooperatives pool member savings and provide loans for new cooperative formation, equipment purchases, or emergency needs. Interest rates are minimal, just enough to cover administrative costs. Decisions about large loans require member votes. Ithilvaeth has the largest credit cooperative serving national needs. Foreign merchants can use these institutions but face higher scrutiny. The system serves collective financial needs without enabling individual wealth hoarding.

**Do people barter or use money?**

Both. Currency is used for inter-cooperative trade, foreign commerce, and purchasing goods from outside the cooperative system. Small personal transactions may involve barter. Within cooperatives, members often exchange favors and services informally without currency. However, profit distributions and major transactions require currency for clarity and fairness. The gold standard facilitates trade with other kingdoms.

**If money is used, is it valuable itself or is it fiat currency?**

Valuable itself. Coins are worth their metal content (gold, silver, copper). Vanguard Reach mints minimal coinage for foreign trade but primarily uses coins from other kingdoms. The gold standard is universally accepted across Eldara.

**If there is fiat money, who guarantees it?**

N/A

**What are people's attitudes toward money?**

Pragmatic and collective-focused. Money is a tool for facilitating trade and measuring cooperative success, not a measure of personal worth. Accumulating personal wealth is viewed with suspicion - it suggests someone is profiting at the collective's expense. Generosity and contribution to the cooperative are valued over personal riches. The wealthy are respected only if they use resources to benefit the whole. Hoarding money is seen as selfishness and counter to the martial values of shared sacrifice.

**What are people's attitudes toward poverty?**

Viewed as a systemic failure rather than individual fault. The cooperative system is designed to prevent destitution - everyone who contributes receives a share. Poverty usually indicates inability to work (injury, disability, age), which triggers community support through veteran services and cooperative aid. Someone who refuses to contribute to any cooperative is seen as lazy and may face social ostracism, but true need is met without judgment. The culture believes no veteran or worker should lack necessities after serving the collective.

**Are there generally acceptable standards for coins?**

Gold standard is universal across Eldara. Coins are valued by weight and metal content. Vanguard Reach accepts foreign coinage based on metal value. Their minimal domestic minting focuses on standardized weights for trade convenience. Cooperative credit unions verify coin authenticity. The system is straightforward - metal weight determines value regardless of whose face is stamped on it.

**How easy and common is counterfeiting?**

Relatively uncommon. Coins valued by metal content rather than face value makes counterfeiting less profitable. Credit cooperatives have members trained in detecting clipped coins or metal cores. The tight-knit cooperative structure makes large-scale fraud difficult - everyone knows who's spending what. Most economic activity happens through cooperative accounting rather than individual coin exchanges, limiting opportunities. Foreign merchants face more scrutiny and their coins are weighed more carefully.

**What does this country import? Export?**

**Exports:** Weapons, armor, and quality steel tools (finest human-made in Eldara). Quarried stone (limestone, granite) for roads and construction. Mercenary warriors for protection contracts. Battle-mages for magical enforcement under Pact. Military advisors and fortification engineers. Combat training programs. Veteran mercenary companies. Armorer services for maintenance and custom work.

**Imports:** Food and livestock from Lenthir (supplements domestic production). Fine goods and luxuries from Aelarion. Forge materials and metals from Caernast, Ironveil, and Iron Hills (iron ore is sparse locally). Ale and wine from the Greenfell. Exotic materials not available domestically. The kingdom can feed itself in good years but prefers to import grain and focus forge capacity on military production.

**How important is trade to the economy?**

Critical. Vanguard Reach cannot be self-sufficient - they lack sufficient iron ore and agricultural land. Their entire economic model depends on exporting military goods and services to purchase necessities. The forge cooperatives require steady imports of raw materials. Food security relies on Lenthir's grain. However, this dependence is strategic strength rather than weakness - every kingdom needs their weapons, creating stable demand. Trade maintains both economic prosperity and diplomatic relationships.

**How is currency exchange handled, and by whom?**

Credit cooperatives handle currency exchange, employing members trained in assessing metal content and current exchange rates. They charge minimal fees covering administrative costs. Major trading centers have dedicated exchange services. Foreign merchants can exchange currency at cooperative offices in Ithilvaeth and border towns. The system is transparent - rates are posted publicly and updated based on metal market values. Since coins are valued by weight, exchange is straightforward calculation rather than speculation.

**What is monetary system used, if any, and who mints it?**

Gold standard with coins valued by metal content. Vanguard Reach mints minimal coinage - just enough for foreign trade convenience and national prestige. Most domestic transactions use foreign coins from Lenthir, Caernast, Aelarion, and other kingdoms. The state mint in Ithilvaeth produces standardized gold, silver, and copper coins bearing the royal seal, but production is limited. The cooperative system doesn't require extensive coinage since much internal accounting happens through cooperative credit systems.

## Legal System & Crime

**What are considered normal and legal ways of gathering evidence and determining guilt?**

Physical evidence, witness testimony, and investigation by magistrates or military investigators. Zone of Truth is available for serious crimes due to the abundance of clerics and paladins in Ithilvaeth, though it requires magistrate authorization and proper justification under Pact guidelines. Military investigators are trained in evidence collection and interrogation. Cooperative councils may provide character witnesses. The accused has the right to present counter-evidence and call witnesses. Confessions alone are insufficient without corroborating evidence - the military culture values proof over assumption.

**Are torture and magic part of the legal system?**

Torture is not standard practice and is considered dishonorable. The military code emphasizes that reliable information comes from proper investigation, not coercion. Zone of Truth and other divine magic are used for high-profile cases where truth must be established beyond doubt. However, even magical testimony requires corroboration. The Pact restricts excessive magical use, so truth spells are reserved for serious crimes. Military discipline may involve harsh physical punishment, but systematic torture for evidence is prohibited.

**Are arbitrary judgements by a lord or landowner allowed, or is there a standard that they are supposed to follow?**

No arbitrary judgments. All citizens, regardless of rank, are subject to military justice following established codes. Magistrates and military courts must follow legal procedure. Cooperative councils cannot arbitrarily punish members - expulsion requires documented cause and hearing. Even the King cannot override established military law without Parliament involvement. The system emphasizes rule of law over individual authority, reflecting the collective nature of society.

**Is there an appeal system?**

Yes. Local military courts can be appealed to regional military tribunals, then to the High Magistrate's court in Ithilvaeth. The most serious cases can be appealed to the King, particularly those involving military honor or capital punishment. Magical crimes under Pact jurisdiction can be appealed to the Tribunal of Balance. The system allows three levels of appeal for most cases. Appeals must present new evidence or demonstrate procedural error.

**How high can a case go in the system before it is finally settled?**

Local military court → Regional military tribunal → High Magistrate's court (Ithilvaeth) → The King (for capital cases or matters of military honor). Magical crimes under Pact jurisdiction follow parallel path ending at the Tribunal of Balance. Four levels maximum for most cases. The King's judgment is final for domestic matters. The system balances efficient military justice with proper oversight.

**Is everyone tried in the same courts or are there special courts for special classes of people — for example, are mages tried in specialized wizard's court?**

Everyone goes through the military justice system regardless of rank or cooperative membership. There are no separate noble courts - the egalitarian culture treats all citizens equally before the law. The only exception is magical crimes, which are handled by Pact enforcement agencies with jurisdiction over practitioners. Military rank may affect sentencing considerations (officers held to higher standards) but not which court hears the case.

**Are there separate courts for civil and criminal matters?**

No formal separation. Military courts handle both criminal offenses and civil disputes. Cooperative councils mediate internal disputes first, but if resolution fails, cases go to military magistrates. The military justice system addresses everything from murder to property disputes between cooperatives. This unified approach reflects the military structure permeating all aspects of society.

**Are there separate courts for magical and non-magical matters?**

Yes. Magical crimes and Pact violations are handled by external enforcement agencies under treaty obligations. All other matters go through military courts. The division is clear: if magic is involved in the crime itself, enforcement contractors have jurisdiction. If a mage commits non-magical crime, military courts handle it.

**Are there separate courts for humans and non-humans?**

No. All residents face the same military justice system. The few non-humans living in Vanguard Reach (visiting merchants, dwarven metalworkers, etc.) are subject to the same laws and courts as citizens. Treaties with other kingdoms may allow their nationals to be tried in their home jurisdictions for minor offenses, but serious crimes are prosecuted locally.

**Are there separate courts for religious matters?**

No. Religious disputes are handled through the same military court system if they involve actual crimes or civil violations. Purely theological disagreements within religious communities are internal matters. The state doesn't intervene in religious doctrine, but crimes committed in the name of religion receive no special treatment.

**What things are considered truly serious crimes and why (example: a trade-oriented culture might consider counterfeiting or bootlegging a death-penalty crime while in a place where life is cheap murder might be something that only results in a small fine)?**

Murder, as it destroys what the community invested in training and protecting. Desertion or cowardice during mandatory service - betraying the collective's trust. Sabotaging cooperatives or stealing from collective resources - attacking the foundation of society. Treason and collaboration with enemies. Hoarding resources or fraud that enriches individuals at cooperative expense. Magical crimes endangering communities. These crimes violate either martial honor or collective welfare, the two pillars of Vanguard Reach culture.

**What are the punishments for serious versus minor crimes?**

Serious crimes: Death by execution, long-term imprisonment, or hard labor battalions. Murder, desertion, and treason typically result in death. Cooperative fraud may result in labor battalions with assets seized for restitution. Minor crimes: Fines paid to victims or cooperatives, public reprimand, temporary loss of cooperative membership, corporal punishment (flogging for military infractions), community service, mandatory retraining. Medium offenses: Extended labor service, significant fines, demotion in military rank, expulsion from cooperatives.

**Are there prisons, or are people punished and released?**

Yes, prisons exist for violent offenders and those awaiting trial. Long-term imprisonment is used for serious crimes where execution seems excessive but the criminal cannot be trusted in society. Labor battalions serve as alternative to imprisonment - criminals work on dangerous or difficult projects (mining, fortification construction, road building) under military guard. Most minor criminals are punished and released. The system emphasizes either elimination of threats (execution), containment (prison), productive punishment (labor battalions), or swift correction (corporal punishment/fines).

**Are there degrees of punishment such as fines, branding, public whippings, removal of body parts, executions — or do they just hang everybody regardless of the crime?**

Yes. Fines and restitution for property crimes. Public reprimand for dishonor. Flogging for military infractions and some medium offenses. Execution by hanging or beheading for capital crimes. Branding and mutilation are not practiced - the culture values physical capability and views permanent disfigurement as wasteful destruction of a potentially productive citizen. Even criminals can be rehabilitated into cooperative work after serving punishment. Execution is reserved for those deemed irredeemable threats.

**Who is responsible for catching criminals?**

Military police patrol cities and enforce laws. Local cooperative councils report crimes and may detain suspects. Citizens are expected to intervene when witnessing crimes - the collective responsibility culture means bystanders who ignore crimes may face charges themselves. Military investigators handle serious cases. Magical crimes are investigated by Pact enforcement contractors. The King's Guard handles threats to the royal family. Everyone has duty to maintain order.

**Who pays the thief takers?**

Military police are funded through taxation as part of the standing military. Investigators are government employees. Pact enforcement contractors are paid through kingdom contracts. There are no private bounty hunters - the state handles law enforcement. Citizens who assist in capturing criminals may receive commendation but not payment. The collective system assumes law enforcement is a shared duty.

**Who pays for prisons and jails?**

State taxation funds all prisons and detention facilities. Labor battalions are partially self-funding through their productive work (mining, construction), with profits going to victim restitution or state coffers. The comprehensive tax system covers all aspects of justice infrastructure. No private prisons exist - incarceration is state responsibility.

**Who supplies food to prisoners?**

State provides basic rations from agricultural cooperatives. Prisoners receive adequate nutrition to maintain work capacity but no luxuries. Labor battalion prisoners eat the same field rations as soldiers on campaign. Family members may supplement with personal food deliveries for imprisoned relatives. The system ensures survival but not comfort.

**How are law enforcement officers organized?**

Military police operate as units within the standing army structure, with clear ranks and chain of command. Each major city has a garrison responsible for law enforcement. Regional commanders coordinate multiple garrisons. The High Magistrate oversees the entire system nationally. Local cooperatives have designated members who report crimes and assist military police. The organization mirrors military structure - disciplined, hierarchical, and efficient.

**Are there independent precincts, overlapping districts, or separate jurisdictions?**

Clear jurisdictional boundaries. Each city has a designated military garrison with defined territory. Regional commands coordinate between garrisons but don't overlap authority. Cooperative councils handle initial dispute mediation but have no arrest powers. The only overlapping jurisdiction is magical crimes, where Pact enforcement agencies supersede military police. The military structure prevents jurisdictional confusion.

**Can law enforcement be hired?**

No. All law enforcement is state-run through military police. Private security doesn't exist - it would contradict the collective model and create inequality where the wealthy could buy protection. Cooperatives may designate internal security members, but they have no legal authority beyond cooperative property. Foreign merchants traveling through Vanguard Reach must rely on state protection or travel with military escort contracts.

**Are there lawyers or advocates?**

Yes, but structured differently than private practice. Advocates are trained through the High Magistrate's office and assigned to cases, particularly for accused who cannot represent themselves effectively. Both prosecution and defense have access to trained advocates. Senior cooperative members may speak as character witnesses or help members understand proceedings, but formal advocacy requires certification. The system ensures fair representation regardless of wealth, reflecting egalitarian values.

**Who can afford legal representation?**

Everyone. Legal advocacy is provided by the state as part of the justice system. No one pays advocates directly - they're government employees funded through taxation. This prevents wealth from determining justice outcomes. However, prominent cooperative leaders or military officers may have better access to experienced advocates through connections, creating some informal advantage despite official equality.

**Who trains the legal experts and are they certified?**

The High Magistrate's office runs a training program combining apprenticeship with formal study of legal codes and military justice. Candidates must have completed military service and demonstrate aptitude. Certification requires passing comprehensive examinations and practical trial experience under supervision. Continuing education is mandatory. The system produces competent advocates but also ensures they understand military culture and cooperative principles.

**Are people guilty until proven innocent, innocent until proven guilty, or does it depend on the mood the bench is in when the case comes in front of it?**

Innocent until proven guilty in principle. The military justice system requires evidence beyond reasonable doubt. However, the collective culture means someone caught by multiple witnesses or in obvious circumstances faces an uphill battle. Judges are trained to follow procedure but human nature and military discipline culture can make the accused feel presumed guilty. The system strives for fairness but values efficiency.

**Are there assumptions made about how an accused criminals will be treated?**

Accused criminals are detained if they're flight risks or violent threats. Non-violent offenders may remain in their cooperatives under supervision pending trial. Treatment during detention is harsh but not abusive - military discipline standards apply. The accused retain rights to advocates and fair hearings. However, the military culture means discipline is strict and conditions spartan. Those accused of dishonoring military service or betraying cooperatives face social ostracism even before conviction.

**Are there judges other than the nobles or gentry?**

Yes. Judges come from career military officers and trained magistrates, not nobility. The monarchy is hereditary but judicial positions are merit-based. Most judges rose through military ranks or the High Magistrate's training program. Social class doesn't determine judicial appointment - competence, fairness, and understanding of military law do. This reflects the egalitarian cooperative culture where ability matters more than birth.

**How are judges paid and by whom?**

State taxation funds all judicial salaries. Judges are government employees receiving standard compensation based on rank and experience. Payment doesn't come from local communities or cooperatives, ensuring judges remain impartial. Senior judges in Ithilvaeth earn more than local magistrates, but even this follows standardized scales. No private payment or bribes - corruption of judges is treated as serious betrayal of collective trust.

**How often are remote areas likely to see a judge?**

Quarterly for most areas. Circuit judges travel to smaller communities on regular schedules. Remote military outposts have designated officers who serve judicial functions between circuit visits. Serious cases may be transported to regional garrisons for immediate judgment. The military structure ensures even remote areas have access to justice, though it may not be immediate. Emergency cases can request priority judicial assignment.

**Is mob justice common? Is it legal?**

Rare and illegal. The military discipline culture emphasizes following proper procedures. Citizens are expected to detain suspects and turn them over to military police, not administer punishment themselves. However, in combat situations or when defending cooperatives from immediate threats, citizens may use necessary force. Mob justice that bypasses the legal system is prosecuted - it undermines military authority and collective order. The culture of shared responsibility means people intervene, but through proper channels.

**How is mob justice viewed by society?**

Viewed as dishonorable failure to trust the system. The military culture emphasizes discipline and following command structures, mob action suggests lack of faith in military justice. It's seen as chaos threatening collective order. However, immediate defense of cooperatives or comrades under attack is distinguished from mob justice after the fact. Society expects intervention during crimes but judgment through proper courts.

**Are highwaymen, muggers, bandits, or pirates common or rare?**

Rare. The pervasive military culture means most citizens are combat-trained and roads are regularly patrolled by military police. Bandits face armed, disciplined opposition. The cooperative system reduces desperate poverty that drives crime. Cooperative councils track their members, making it difficult to operate criminal enterprises. Border regions with other kingdoms see occasional smugglers, but organized banditry is nearly nonexistent.

**What sorts of crimes would the average citizen be likely encounter in their lifetime?**

Cooperative disputes (theft of tools, profit distribution arguments), minor assaults during drunken disputes, property damage, fraud in personal transactions, desertion or dereliction during military service, domestic violence, occasional theft from cooperatives. Most citizens will never witness murder or serious violence. The tight-knit cooperative structure makes major crimes rare but petty disputes common. Military infractions during mandatory service are nearly universal minor offenses.

**Who can make or repeal laws?**

Parliament creates and repeals civilian laws through democratic vote. The King can issue military codes and decrees related to defense, but major legal changes require Parliament approval. Cooperative councils can establish internal rules but cannot contradict kingdom law. The High Magistrate advises on legal consistency. Emergency wartime powers allow the King to issue temporary decrees, but these expire when the emergency ends unless Parliament ratifies them. The system balances monarchical military authority with democratic civilian governance.

**How are alleged criminals treated before their trials?**

Detained if violent or flight risks, confined in military holding facilities. Non-violent offenders may remain in cooperatives under supervision and monitoring. Treatment is harsh but not cruel - military discipline standards with adequate food and basic necessities. Advocates can visit freely. Family visits are permitted. Those accused of serious crimes face isolation to prevent witness intimidation or escape. The accused retain legal rights but experience strict military discipline during detention.

**How are convicted criminals treated?**

Punishment is administered swiftly. Minor offenders face flogging, fines, or public reprimand then return to cooperatives. Labor battalion prisoners work under military guard on difficult projects - treated as disciplined workers, not tortured. Long-term prisoners receive basic necessities but harsh conditions. Executed criminals receive military burial if they served honorably before their crime. Reformed criminals who complete sentences can rejoin cooperatives, though stigma persists. The focus is correction or elimination, not ongoing punishment.

**Do the police, military, or city guard make a practise of roughing up suspects?**

Not systematically. Military police are disciplined and follow procedures. Excessive force during arrest is punishable unless the suspect violently resists. However, the military culture values toughness - suspects who resist or show disrespect may face harsher treatment within legal bounds. Brutality that leaves permanent injury or prevents fair trial is prosecuted. The line between firm military discipline and abuse is clear in regulations but sometimes blurred in practice.

**Are there laws forbidding certain types of people (peasants, wizards, priests, women) from carrying arms?**

No. All citizens undergo mandatory military training and are expected to maintain combat readiness. Carrying weapons is normal and encouraged. Convicted violent criminals may be forbidden weapons as part of sentencing. The egalitarian culture means gender, class, or profession doesn't restrict weapon ownership. Even foreign visitors can carry arms though they face scrutiny from military police. The society assumes armed, trained citizens contribute to collective defense.

**Are there laws requiring certain people to be skilled with certain weapons?**

Universal military service requires all citizens to achieve basic competency with standard military weapons during training. Beyond this baseline, no specific requirements exist. Forge cooperative members receive additional training with weapons they produce to understand quality standards. Officers are expected to maintain higher proficiency. The culture creates social pressure to maintain skills, but only the mandatory service requirement is legally enforced.

**Are certain spells (as opposed to magic generally) illegal?**

Yes, following the Pact of the Ythraewyn. Mind control, necromancy, destructive magic without defensive justification, and frivolous magic use are illegal. The state magical colleges enforce these restrictions strictly. Battle-mages are licensed for combat spells under military authority. Healing and protective divine magic by clerics and paladins is broadly permitted. Violations are handled by Pact enforcement contractors with Tribunal jurisdiction.

**How would a criminal magician be detected? Apprehended? Punished?**

Detected by state magical college monitors who track magical use and investigate reports. Large-scale illegal magic triggers alerts. Military police coordinate with Pact enforcement contractors for apprehension. Battle-mages assist in capturing dangerous practitioners. Punishment ranges from magical binding for serious violations, to fines and restrictions for minor infractions, to death for extremely dangerous uses. The centralized magical colleges make monitoring more effective than in kingdoms with scattered practitioners.

**Which nations have formal relations with this country?**

[[Kingdom of Lenthir]] (strongest alliance, mutual defense and trade), [[Aelarion]] (loose diplomatic ties based on ancient Second Era treaties), [[Caernast]] (trade partner as a proxy for [[Khalgrod Deep]]), [[Ironveil]] (trade for quality metals tools). **Who can be ambassadors and envoys?**

The Diplomatic Envoy (appointed position reporting to the King) handles routine foreign correspondence. For important negotiations, the King appoints special envoys from senior military officers or Parliament members with relevant expertise. No professional diplomatic corps exists. Envoys must have military service background and demonstrate understanding of both martial culture and cooperative economics. Appointments are temporary and task-specific.

**Are there standing embassies and consulates, or are envoys sent only when something specific comes up?**

No standing embassies. The military culture views permanent diplomatic missions as wasteful when regular correspondence and trade relationships suffice. Special envoys are sent for treaty negotiations, crisis mediation, or major trade agreements. Trade representatives from cooperatives maintain informal relationships with foreign partners but have no diplomatic authority. The King and Diplomatic Envoy coordinate foreign policy from Ithilvaeth.

**How are treaties arranged?**

Direct negotiation between the King and foreign heads of state, with Parliament approval required for trade agreements affecting cooperatives or taxation. Military treaties are primarily royal prerogative with War Council input. Treaties are practical documents focused on specific issues: mutual defense terms, trade tariffs, border security coordination. Parliament debates economic treaties extensively since they affect cooperative operations. The process balances monarchical foreign policy authority with democratic oversight of economic impacts.

**Are there any significant ones currently in force or coming up for signing?**

The mutual defense pact with Lenthir remains the cornerstone, guaranteeing military support in exchange for favorable grain prices. Ancient treaties with Aelarion from the Second Era establish magical cooperation and Pact enforcement coordination, though these are rarely invoked. Trade agreements with Caernast govern machinery imports and tariffs. An aging border security arrangement with Lenthir coordinates responses to threats from [[Gruz'Thok]] and [[Durmshaven]] orc territories. No major treaties are pending, though some Parliament members debate renegotiating Lenthir grain prices as agricultural productivity improves.

**How much do official attitudes toward other countries affect commerce and trade?**

Moderately. The cooperative system means trade is coordinated by councils rather than individual merchants, so state policy directly affects commerce. Poor diplomatic relations could halt trade if Parliament votes to restrict it. However, the pragmatic military culture recognizes that trade maintains strength - cutting off essential imports like grain or iron ore would be strategic foolishness. Economic necessity usually overrides political tensions, but cooperatives can collectively boycott kingdoms for ethical or strategic reasons.

**Do merchants pretty much ignore tensions between governments as long as they can make a profit, or will this get them into trouble?**

Cooperative representatives cannot ignore government policy like independent merchants might. Trade decisions require worker council votes and must align with kingdom interests. A cooperative trading with an enemy during conflict would face prosecution for treason. However, during peacetime, cooperatives have some autonomy in choosing trade partners within legal bounds. Foreign merchants visiting Vanguard Reach must navigate both official policy and cooperative preferences - political tensions mean fewer willing trading partners even if trade remains technically legal.

**How much formal spying and intelligence gathering is normally done by governments?**

Moderate level. Military intelligence focuses on potential threats - monitoring neighboring territories for military buildups, tracking orc and giant clan movements, assessing other kingdoms' military capabilities. The War Council maintains an intelligence division. However, resources are limited and focus is primarily defensive. Economic intelligence comes naturally through cooperative trade relationships. Forge workers know what other kingdoms purchase. Political espionage is minimal, the military culture views sneaking and deception as less honorable than direct strength.

**How much spying is done by the military?**

Military intelligence is the primary espionage arm. Scout units patrol borders and gather tactical information. Officers traveling abroad observe foreign military capabilities and training methods. Veterans who take mercenary contracts report back on foreign armies. The intelligence division analyzes threats systematically. However, the focus is military assessment, not political manipulation. Deep-cover spies are rare, the culture values direct strength over subterfuge. Most intelligence comes from open observation, veteran reports, and analysis rather than covert operations.

**Do merchants (or companies) engage in espionage?**

Cooperative representatives gather economic intelligence naturally through trade relationships learning what other kingdoms need, what they're willing to pay, production capabilities. This information flows back to worker councils and Parliament. However, systematic industrial espionage is rare. The cooperative structure means workers share information for collective benefit rather than personal profit, reducing motivation for secretive theft. Forge cooperatives do study foreign techniques but through legitimate exchange rather than spying. The egalitarian culture makes clandestine behavior socially suspect.

**Who has the best information gathering system?**

 Vanguard Reach's military intelligence is competent but focused narrowly on defense. Their strength is analyzing military threats, not political machinations. The cooperative transparency that strengthens internal society makes covert operations culturally difficult.

Lenthir is the closest and most essential ally. The relationship is economic symbiosis and military cooperation. Vanguard Reach provides protection and weapons, Lenthir provides food. Aelarion maintains friendly relations based on Second Era treaties and shared commitment to Pact enforcement. These alliances are stable and mutually beneficial, built on complementary strengths rather than temporary political convenience.

None currently among established kingdoms. Historical First Era conflicts are too ancient to create modern rivalry. The main tension is economic competition with dwarven kingdoms (Ironveil, Khalgrod Deep) over metalwork markets. Vanguard Reach produces cheaper human steel, dwarves produce superior quality. This creates commercial competition without political hostility. Some kingdoms view Vanguard Reach's military strength with suspicion, but no kingdom actively opposes them. The orc territories to the south ([[Gruz'Thok]], [[Durmshaven]]), and the Giant kingdoms of the north ([[Hrimgard]], [[Skaldrivik]]) are threats rather than rivals.

**How do these traditional alliances and rivalries affect foreign policy?**

The Lenthir alliance dictates grain import policy and southern border coordination. Maintaining this relationship is paramount since food security depends on it. Friendly Aelarion relations ensure access to magical services under Pact. Economic competition with dwarven metalwork drives forge innovation and pricing strategy. The lack of active rivals allows defensive rather than aggressive foreign policy. Parliament debates whether strong military without enemies wastes resources, but the King maintains readiness as cultural tradition and deterrent.

Less important than military alliances and economic relationships. The current King's marriage to his Prince Consort demonstrates that personal compatibility and political partnership matter more than dynastic alliances. Royal marriages to foreign nobility are rare. The egalitarian culture views arranged marriages as archaic. However, cooperative leaders sometimes marry into prominent families from trade partner kingdoms, creating informal economic ties. Political function takes secondary role to genuine partnership.

Minimally. The cooperative culture values institutional relationships over personal connections. A royal marriage to Lenthir nobility wouldn't dramatically change the alliance, which rests on mutual economic and military need. Parliament debates foreign policy based on collective benefit, not family sentiment. However, personal relationships between rulers can smooth negotiations and build trust. The current King's lack of foreign marriage ties hasn't weakened diplomacy because the military reputation and trade relationships carry more weight.

## National Politics

**Does the level of technological advancement match the level of social and political advancement?**

Yes, reasonably well. Vanguard Reach has advanced metallurgy and forge technology paired with sophisticated socialist political structures. The cooperative economic system requires complex coordination and democratic decision-making that matches their organizational capabilities. Military training is universal, creating a disciplined, educated population capable of participating in governance. The centralized state magical colleges reflect their ability to manage specialized institutions. Technology and social structure evolved together through centuries of collective military culture.

**What are the major political factions at present?**

**Military Passivists**: Advocate reducing military expenditure and societal focus on warfare. Argue that without recent conflicts, resources should shift to civilian needs. Respond to external criticism that Vanguard Reach's military strength is unnecessary provocation. Growing influence in Parliament but face resistance from traditional culture.

**Military Activists**: Believe Vanguard Reach should leverage military superiority more aggressively. Argue that strength without assertion invites weakness and disrespect. Increasingly vocal in War Council and among younger officers. Some influenced by prominent military leaders suggesting the kingdom is viewed as weak despite its power.

**Economic Reformers**: Emerging movement questioning whether successful cooperatives should operate differently. Led by prosperous forge cooperative leaders who argue exceptional performance deserves greater rewards. Subtle push toward allowing more individual wealth accumulation and private enterprise. Growing influence over past 20 years, particularly among economically successful cooperatives.

**Traditional Socialists**: Defend current cooperative system and equitable distribution. Believe collective welfare must supersede individual gain. Maintain that socialist principles and military readiness built the kingdom's strength. Represent older cooperative members and those committed to foundational values.

**How long have the current political factions been around?**

These divisions have sharpened significantly in the past 20 years. Military Passivist vs Activist debate existed in muted form for decades but intensified recently as external kingdoms increasingly question Vanguard Reach's military focus. The Economic Reformer movement is newer, emerging as certain cooperatives achieved exceptional success and their leaders began questioning equal distribution. Traditional Socialists represent the established order that's existed since the First Era. The factional lines were blurry until recent years when debates became more polarized and organized.

**Which factions are allies and which are enemies?**

**Natural Tensions**: Military Activists clash with Military Passivists on fundamental strategy. Economic Reformers conflict with Traditional Socialists on wealth distribution principles.

**Unexpected Alignments**: Some Military Activists align with Economic Reformers, both believing exceptional performance deserves greater rewards (military glory or economic success). Military Passivists sometimes align with Traditional Socialists, both wanting to preserve collective welfare over competitive advantage.

**Cross-Cutting Issues**: The monarchy's legitimacy creates strange bedfellows. Some Traditional Socialists defend the King as symbol of socialist values, while some Economic Reformers join whisper campaigns questioning royal competence, using coded rhetoric about "traditional leadership."

**Unstable Coalitions**: Military Activists and Economic Reformers cooperate tactically but have different end goals (aggressive expansion vs economic liberalization). This alliance is opportunistic rather than philosophical.

**Are there any potential new forces on the political scene?**

**Cooperative Privatization Movement**: Still forming, but voices argue cooperatives should be allowed to convert to private ownership if members vote for it. Currently whispered in taverns and private forge meetings rather than Parliament floors. Leaders claim workers should control their own destinies, including choosing capitalism.

**Anti-Monarchy Faction**: Extremely small but growing. Questions whether hereditary rule fits socialist principles. Uses the King's marriage as example of "royal decadence" divorced from common military service. Not yet organized enough to be true faction but sentiment exists.

**Foreign Trade Reformers**: Argue Vanguard Reach should reduce dependency on Lenthir grain by diversifying suppliers or boosting domestic agriculture. Concerned about price leverage. Still minor but gaining attention as Lenthir's agricultural power grows.

**Radical Egalitarians**: Reaction to Economic Reformers, arguing even current system allows too much inequality. Want stricter profit distribution and more aggressive wealth caps. Very small but vocal in less successful cooperatives.

**How much influence do special interest groups (such as merchants, wizards, or religious sects) have on politics?**

**Forge Cooperatives**: Enormous influence, especially large successful ones producing weapons and armor. Their representatives frequently testify before Parliament on economic policy. The most prosperous forge leaders have become influential voices, hosting gatherings where political discussions occur. Their economic success gives them credibility that translates to political power.

**Military Leadership**: The War Council holds significant influence over the King and shapes military policy directly. Senior officers command respect in Parliament debates. Prominent military leaders can sway public opinion through their reputations, making their political positions carry weight beyond formal authority.

**State Magical Colleges**: Moderate influence focused on Pact enforcement and magical policy. Battle-mage instructors advise on military applications. However, magic serves military needs rather than forming independent political power. Magical practitioners are too integrated into military structure to be separate interest group.

**Religious Groups**: Minimal organized influence. Clerics and paladins are valued for Zone of Truth and divine support but don't operate as political bloc. Religious devotion is personal rather than institutionally powerful. The Alorama and Nyx are respected but temples don't lobby Parliament.

**Agricultural Cooperatives**: Limited influence due to sector's small size. Food import dependency means domestic agriculture isn't politically powerful. Some Agricultural Reformers want to change this.

**Veterans**: Everyone is a veteran, so this isn't a distinct interest group. However, decorated war heroes and those who served with distinction can leverage reputation politically.

**How do interest groups exercise their influence?**

**Forge Cooperatives**: Control economic leverage through production decisions. Large forges can threaten production slowdowns if policies displease them. Cooperative leaders testify before Parliament, host political gatherings, and fund community projects building loyalty. Their success stories are used to argue for policy changes. Worker councils vote as blocs on Parliamentary representatives.

**Military Leadership**: Leverage respect and command authority. Officers who speak politically carry weight because citizens trust military judgment. Control training programs that shape younger generations' views. War Council directly advises King, giving them access other groups lack. Prominent leaders can rally support through speeches invoking martial honor and strength.

**State Magical Colleges**: Advise on technical magical matters where Parliament lacks expertise. Control Pact enforcement implementation, giving them practical power in that domain. Battle-mages demonstrate capabilities that justify budget requests. However, their influence is narrow and technical rather than broad political power.

**What political positions are considered conservative?**

**Strong Socialism**: Maintaining strict cooperative structures and equitable profit distribution. Opposing private ownership or wealth accumulation beyond collective success. Believing the current system built Vanguard Reach's strength and shouldn't be abandoned.

**Military Primacy**: The military culture and universal service are fundamental to identity. Maintaining high defense spending and combat readiness regardless of external peace. Military values (discipline, honor, collective sacrifice) should guide society.

**Traditional Leadership**: Respecting the monarchy and established government structures. Valuing proven hierarchies and chain of command. Opposing radical political experimentation.

**Lenthir Alliance**: Maintaining the essential food security relationship even if prices seem high. Recognizing mutual dependency and honoring long-standing treaties.

**Pact Adherence**: Strictly following magical restrictions and cooperating with enforcement. Viewing the Pact as necessary check on power that could devastate kingdoms.

**What political positions are considered liberal?**

**Economic Liberalization**: Allowing successful cooperatives to reward top performers disproportionately. Permitting private enterprise in sectors beyond personal services. Letting cooperatives convert to private ownership if members vote for it. Reducing wealth distribution requirements to incentivize individual achievement.

**Military Reduction**: Decreasing defense spending and mandatory service length. Shifting resources from military to civilian infrastructure and welfare. Responding to external criticism by becoming less militaristic. Viewing perpetual military readiness without conflicts as wasteful.

**Democratic Expansion**: Increasing Parliamentary power relative to monarchy. Making War Council positions elected rather than appointed. Greater transparency in military decision-making. Questioning whether hereditary rule fits socialist principles.

**Trade Diversification**: Reducing dependency on Lenthir grain by developing domestic agriculture or alternative suppliers. Accepting higher food costs for strategic independence. Expanding trade relationships beyond traditional partners.

**Individual Rights**: Emphasizing personal freedom within collective structure. Protecting minority opinions and lifestyles from majority pressure. Arguing that socialism should allow diverse personal choices, not enforce conformity.

**Are there political positions that are unthinkable?**

**Abolishing Universal Military Service**: The mandatory service is fundamental to identity and egalitarian culture. Even Military Passivists want reduced focus, not eliminated service. Training every citizen is what makes the collective work.

**Complete Privatization**: Dissolving all cooperatives and converting entirely to capitalism would destroy the social foundation. Even Economic Reformers want reform, not total abandonment of cooperative principles.

**Surrendering Military Strength**: Deliberately weakening the army to appease other kingdoms. The military reputation is source of pride and security. Becoming militarily inferior is cultural suicide.

**Breaking Lenthir Alliance**: Despite price tensions, ending food security relationship is strategically insane. No one seriously proposes this, only renegotiation.

**Abandoning Pact Enforcement**: Violating magical restrictions would invite Tribunal sanctions and undermine the stability that ended the Second Era conflicts. Even those who want more magical power don't advocate ignoring the Pact.

**Hereditary Privilege Beyond Monarchy**: Creating noble classes or allowing wealth to become hereditary advantage. The egalitarian military culture absolutely rejects this

**Are there any shaky political alliances between disparate groups?**

**Military Activists and Economic Reformers**: Cooperate tactically because both believe exceptional performance deserves greater rewards (military glory or economic success). However, their goals conflict. Activists want aggressive expansion requiring collective sacrifice and military spending. Reformers want individual wealth accumulation and reduced taxation. This alliance fractures when resource allocation forces choices between military buildup and economic liberalization.

**Military Passivists and Traditional Socialists**: Align on maintaining collective welfare and reducing aggressive posturing. However, Passivists often want to redirect military spending to other programs, while Traditional Socialists want to preserve current distribution including military funding. They disagree on whether the military culture itself needs reduction or just its external focus.

**Economic Reformers and Some Cooperative Leaders**: Large successful forges support reform, but smaller struggling cooperatives fear it would destroy their collective support. The alliance between prosperous and average cooperatives is fragile, held together by rhetoric about "worker choice" but divided by actual economic interests.

**Why were the current alliances formed?**

**Military Activists and Economic Reformers**: United by frustration with status quo and belief that Vanguard Reach isn't using its advantages. Activists see military strength going unused while kingdoms view them as threats. Reformers see cooperative success being diluted by equal distribution. Both believe current system rewards mediocrity over excellence. Influential leaders in both camps have built personal relationships, reinforcing the alliance through shared meals and political strategy sessions.

**Military Passivists and Traditional Socialists**: Formed defensively against the Activist/Reformer alliance. Both recognize that aggressive military expansion and economic liberalization threaten core values. Passivists fear war, Socialists fear inequality, but both oppose the direction their opponents push. The alliance is reactive, built on shared enemies rather than shared vision.

**Economic Reformers and Cooperative Leaders**: Emerged organically as successful forge leaders questioned why exceptional production didn't translate to exceptional rewards. Started as informal discussions at forge gatherings, evolved into coordinated Parliamentary advocacy. The most prosperous cooperatives see reform as recognition of their achievements. They frame it as empowering workers rather than abandoning socialism.

**How long before current alliances fall apart, and, when they do, what will the effects be?**

**Military Activists and Economic Reformers** (5-10 years): Fractures when resource decisions force choices. Military expansion requires taxation and collective sacrifice. Economic liberalization requires reduced taxation and individual accumulation. Currently united by opposition to status quo, but governing would expose incompatible goals. When broken: Could split into multiple smaller factions. Economic policy becomes chaotic as neither camp achieves majority. Military spending becomes unstable political football.

**Military Passivists and Traditional Socialists** (10-20 years): More stable because both want to preserve aspects of current system. However, debates over military budget allocation will strain it. Passivists willing to reduce military more than Socialists comfortable with. When broken: Traditional Socialists might align with moderate Military Activists on maintaining strong defense within socialist framework. Passivists could become isolated unless they build new coalitions.

**Economic Reformers and Cooperative Leaders** (Already Fracturing): Large successful cooperatives drive reform, but as policies get specific, smaller cooperatives realize they'd lose collective support. The alliance is already unstable, held together by charismatic leaders promising everyone benefits. When broken: Bitter class divisions emerge between prosperous and struggling cooperatives. Socialist backlash could be severe.

**First Era Near-Extinction**: Cultural memory of First Era wars when humanity almost destroyed itself. Drives both sides of military debate. Activists argue strength prevents repeat, weakness invites aggression. Passivists argue excessive military focus caused those wars, peace requires restraint. The trauma shapes fundamental assumptions about security.

**Second Era Divine Manipulation**: Memory of how divine powers used kingdoms as proxies. Creates suspicion of external influence and emphasis on independence. **Ancient Vanguard Reach vs Lenthir Conflicts**: Before alliance, First Era border disputes over resources. Creates underlying tension despite current friendship. No. Magical practitioners are integrated into the military and cooperative structure rather than forming independent political power. Battle-mages are military officers first, spellcasters second. State magical colleges are government institutions training mages for collective service, not independent guilds. Clerics and paladins serve religious and military functions but don't organize politically.

Like all citizens, magical practitioners complete mandatory military service and then work within cooperatives or state institutions. They have technical expertise that influences magical policy, Pact enforcement, and military applications, but this is professional input rather than political faction. The cooperative culture prevents magical practitioners from forming separate professional class with independent interests. Magic is a skill set employed for collective benefit, not a source of political identity or power bloc.

## Military & Warfare

**Which peoples, countries, and races have been in conflict with this nation in the recent past?**

Giants wandering south through mountain passes require military response rarely. No formal conflicts with other kingdoms in recent history. The last kingdom-level war was during the Second Era conflicts over 4,000 years ago.

Orc raids target Lenthir primarily, but Vanguard Reach honors mutual defense agreements. These are border security operations rather than existential wars. The conflicts maintain military readiness but don't threaten national survival.

The last true war involving Vanguard Reach was during the Second Era's religious conflicts when divine manipulation nearly tore humanity apart. The kingdom sided with more aggressive responses to threats, building the martial culture that persists today. This ended with [[the Covenant of Divine Order]] establishment around year 1200 of that era. Since the Third Era began 4,000 years ago, no kingdom-level wars have occurred. The extended peace shapes current debates about whether military strength remains necessary.

**Who won the last war?**

No one truly won the Second Era conflicts. Divine manipulation devastated all kingdoms before the Covenant system ended the crisis through institutional cooperation rather than military victory. The lesson learned was that unchecked power destroys everything, leading to current Pact restrictions and diplomatic frameworks. Vanguard Reach emerged with its martial culture intact but chastened by the costs of divine-influenced warfare.

**Are there ongoing tensions from the most recent war?**

Vanguard Reach produces the finest human-made steel weapons in Eldara. Infantry use spears, swords, shields, and crossbows. Cavalry units employ lances and sabers. Siege equipment includes trebuchets, ballistae, and battering rams. The forge cooperatives constantly refine metallurgy and weapon design. Quality approaches dwarven work at lower cost. Limited magical weapons exist due to Pact restrictions. Divine support comes from clerics and paladins integrated throughout forces. Battle-mages are sparse but available for critical operations.

Moderately. Divine magic from clerics and paladins is integral to planning. Healing allows sustained operations, blessings boost morale and effectiveness, Zone of Truth prevents espionage. Battle-mages provide limited offensive capability but their scarcity means commanders don't rely on them. Most tactics assume conventional warfare with divine support. Anti-magic doctrine involves rapid assault to close with enemy casters before sustained spellcasting. The disciplined formations and combined arms approach reflects fighting without magical superiority.

**Do army commanders have to use specific formations or techniques to deal with possible magical attacks?**

Yes. Dispersed formations reduce area-effect spell damage. Rapid maneuver prevents becoming static targets. Shield walls and cover usage deny line-of-sight for targeted spells. Battle-mages and clerics maintain counter-magic readiness. Siege equipment targets enemy spellcasters first. **Clerics and Paladins**: Divine support is standard. Healing maintains combat effectiveness, blessings enhance formations before engagement, protective wards on commanders and critical positions, divine smiting for shock assaults. **Battle-Mages**: Sparse due to Pact restrictions but devastating when deployed. Area denial through destructive spells, counter-magic against enemy casters, reconnaissance through scrying (with proper authorization), communication over distance during operations. **Druids**: Rarely integrated. Some serve in border defense manipulating terrain or calling natural allies, but their scarcity and independence from military hierarchy makes them unreliable.

**Tactical Integration**: Magic supplements conventional forces rather than replacing them. Commanders plan assuming divine support but not relying on battle-magic. The system works because universal training means every soldier understands their role without magical dependency.

**Standing Professional Army**: Approximately 20,000 active duty soldiers organized into Legions (5,000 troops), Cohorts (500 troops), and Companies (100 troops). Clear hierarchical command. Stationed at major fortifications and garrison cities. Always maintained and ready.

**Unit Composition**: Combined arms approach. Each Legion contains heavy infantry, light infantry, cavalry, archers, siege equipment, and integrated divine support (clerics/paladins). Battle-mages assigned to critical operations rather than standard units.

**Special Forces**: Elite units for specific missions. Mountain warfare specialists, siege engineers, naval marines (small force), intelligence operatives.

Formal and independent. The professional standing army has clear chain of command based on rank and merit, not aristocratic privilege. Even during reserve mobilization, recalled citizens enter existing military structure under appointed commanders rather than following cooperative leaders. The King commands overall strategy through the War Council, but tactical execution follows military hierarchy. This reflects the egalitarian culture where competence determines authority.

The standing army maintains 20,000 active troops continuously. Reserve call-up fills ranks from citizens under 35 who completed mandatory service. Recalled reserves enter at their last held rank or are reassigned based on current needs and abilities. Cooperative councils coordinate which members can be spared without crippling production. Medical exemptions are granted for injury or disability. The universal service system means reserve mobilization provides trained soldiers immediately rather than raw recruits.

**Are there professional soldiers and mercenaries?**

The 20,000 standing army are professional career soldiers who chose military service beyond mandatory term. They receive ongoing training, better equipment access, and serve as backbone during mobilization. Vanguard Reach doesn't employ foreign mercenaries and views selling military service as dishonorable. However, individual veterans who completed service may take protection contracts abroad, and foreign kingdoms frequently recruit Vanguard Reach veterans for training and leadership roles. These aren't formal mercenary companies but individual arrangements.

**Is a career in the army possible, or would one have to be a mercenary in order to make a living as a soldier?**

Yes, military career is possible and respected. After completing mandatory 5-year service at 18-23, citizens can apply for professional positions in the standing army. Selection is competitive based on performance during mandatory service. Career soldiers receive steady pay through the socialist system, housing, and veteran benefits. Officers can serve entire lives. The military provides clear advancement path through merit-based promotion. This is one of the most prestigious career options available.

**Does the army accept volunteers?**

Yes. All citizens must complete mandatory 5-year service starting at 18, so there's no need for volunteers to fill basic ranks. However, volunteering for the professional standing army after mandatory service is how the 20,000-strong force is maintained. Elite units, specialized roles, and officer tracks accept volunteers who demonstrate exceptional ability. The cultural prestige of military service means competition is intense. Volunteers for dangerous assignments or extended deployments receive recognition but not additional pay under the equal compensation system.

**How large is a typical army?**

**Standing Force**: 20,000 professional soldiers always maintained, distributed across garrison cities and fortifications. This is the "typical" peacetime army.

**Border Operations**: 500-2,000 troops for giant incursions or coordinated orc responses with Lenthir.

**Full Mobilization**: Could theoretically field 100,000+ by activating all reserves under 35, but this would devastate the cooperative economy. Realistic sustained force: 50,000-75,000 for extended campaigns.

**Training Exercises**: Quarterly reserve training involves 2,000-5,000 citizens practicing coordination.

The 20,000 standing army is already enormous compared to most kingdoms. Other nations view this permanent force as intimidating and unnecessary.

**In an army, what percentage of the soldiers will be trained and what portion will be untrained recruits?**

100% of any mobilized force is trained. Universal mandatory service means every soldier completed 5-year training program. Even reserves maintain quarterly training. No untrained recruits exist. The standing army has highest training level (continuous practice), reserves have good training (regular refreshers), but even the least prepared reserve has more training than typical conscripts in other kingdoms. This universal competence is major advantage and why Vanguard Reach is considered militarily superior.

**Are recruits and conscripts given training, or are they expected to learn on the battlefield?**

All citizens receive comprehensive 5-year training starting at 18 as mandatory service. Training includes weapons proficiency, formations, tactics, physical conditioning, battlefield medicine basics, unit cohesion exercises, and military culture indoctrination. Instructors are experienced professional soldiers. Training facilities exist in every major city. No one enters battlefield untrained. After mandatory service, those joining the standing army receive advanced specialized training. The system produces consistently skilled soldiers rather than learning through combat casualties.

**How is the army supplied?**

Forge cooperatives produce weapons and armor through coordinated production quotas. Agricultural cooperatives provide food rations. The Quartermaster Corps manages logistics through centralized planning. Supply depots positioned strategically throughout the kingdom. During mobilization, cooperatives shift production to military needs through Parliamentary coordination. Transport uses both military logistics units and cooperative resources. The socialist system allows rapid economic conversion to war footing since cooperatives already coordinate collectively. Standing army maintains permanent supply lines, reserves draw from cooperative stockpiles when activated.

**Are soldiers allowed to live off the land and peasantry, or do they pay for what they take?**

Soldiers are forbidden from requisitioning civilian property without compensation. The cooperative culture means taking from collective resources without authorization is theft, even for military. During operations, supply trains provide necessities. If troops must purchase from local cooperatives, military budget covers costs at fair prices. Looting is severely punished as betrayal of socialist principles. Foreign campaigns would establish supply contracts with local populations. The discipline and shared cultural values prevent the predatory relationship between armies and civilians common in other kingdoms.

**How are supplies handled during long campaigns?**

The standing army has established logistics doctrine: supply trains follow combat units, depots positioned along operation routes, cooperative councils coordinate production increases, Quartermaster Corps manages distribution. However, the 4,000-year peace means these systems are untested at scale. Theoretical planning is thorough but actual execution during sustained campaign is unknown. The cooperative economy should adapt quickly to military needs, but coordinating hundreds of thousands of troops over extended operations remains theoretical exercise rather than proven capability.

Professional standing army units carry 5-7 days of rations and essential supplies when mobile. Supply trains extend this to 2-3 weeks for sustained operations, but require established routes and depots. The socialist coordination theoretically allows rapid resupply, but mobile campaigns far from friendly territory would face serious logistics challenges. **What are the accepted conventions for making war (examples: only fight in winter when nobody is busy with crops; don't make war on civilians; only certain kinds of weapons are used)?**

**Proportional Response**: Military force matches threat level. Excessive destruction or unnecessary casualties are dishonorable.

**Seasonal Considerations**: Avoiding campaigns during critical agricultural periods when possible, though professional army allows more flexibility than kingdoms dependent on farmer-soldiers.

**Divine Respect**: Clerics and paladins on both sides may negotiate truces for wounded recovery or proper burial rites.

Yes. Orc tribes follow different honor codes emphasizing personal combat prowess over formation discipline. Giants operate as individuals rather than organized forces, requiring different tactical responses. Other human kingdoms generally share similar conventions due to common cultural heritage and Covenant influence. Dwarven military doctrine is unknown but assumed to be highly disciplined. **Orcs**: Favor heavy weapons (great axes, mauls, two-handed swords) that leverage their strength. Their combat culture emphasizes overwhelming power over finesse. Crude but effective armor and shields that lighter races couldn't effectively wield.

Superior to other human kingdoms in metallurgy and forge techniques. However, still inferior to true dwarven work from Ironveil and other dwarven holds. No significant innovations. The 4,000-year peace means military technology has stagnated. Forge cooperatives refine existing techniques rather than revolutionary breakthroughs. Some Economic Reformers argue the cooperative structure prevents innovation since radical ideas require collective approval rather than individual risk-taking. Battle-mages provide limited capability but are too rare for reliable planning. Anti-magic tactics exist but remain theoretical. Other kingdoms likely integrate magic differently. ## National Infrastructure

**How are roads built in this country?**

Mix of cooperative labor and military engineering. Road construction projects are coordinated by Parliament with worker councils providing labor. Military engineers design major routes for strategic mobility. Abundant stone and concrete materials from quarries allow quality construction. Roads are built wide and solid for military transport. Cooperative construction crews work on scheduled projects with clear quotas. The socialist system allows efficient resource allocation without competing private interests. Military roads receive priority funding and maintenance.

**What state are the roads in?**

Good to excellent on major routes. Stone and concrete construction withstands heavy use. Well-maintained through cooperative labor programs during non-winter months. Winter weather causes wear that requires spring repairs, but this is anticipated and budgeted. Secondary roads are adequate. Remote routes can deteriorate but are less critical given limited settlements. Overall road quality reflects centralized planning and military necessity for reliable transportation.

**Who pays for roads?**

State taxation funds all road construction and maintenance. No tolls or local funding variations. The centralized system ensures uniform quality across the kingdom regardless of regional wealth. Military roads receive guaranteed funding. Cooperative labor provides workforce but is compensated through regular wages. Resource allocation comes from national budget priorities established by Parliament.

**Who maintains the roads?**

Cooperative work crews assigned to infrastructure maintenance. Each region has designated cooperatives responsible for their sections. Military engineers supervise major routes. Spring repair season after winter damage is coordinated nationally. Citizens may be called for emergency repairs during mandatory service. The system rotates maintenance duties among cooperatives to distribute burden fairly.

**Are there good roads? Who builds them?**

Yes, excellent roads on strategic routes. Built by cooperative labor under military engineer supervision. Stone-paved or concrete major routes connecting garrison cities and Ithilvaeth. The combination of abundant materials, centralized planning, military necessity, and disciplined workforce produces superior infrastructure compared to most kingdoms. Construction follows standardized designs ensuring consistency. The socialist system prevents corner-cutting since cooperatives don't profit from cheaper construction.

**Are there tolls?**

No tolls anywhere. The socialist principle is that infrastructure serves collective benefit and shouldn't create barriers to movement or commerce. All roads are funded through general taxation. Free movement of goods and people maximizes economic efficiency. Military mobility requires unimpeded access. The concept of charging citizens to use collectively-built roads contradicts foundational values.

**Are roads guarded or patrolled?**

Yes, regularly. Military police patrol major routes on scheduled circuits. Garrison cities send patrols along connecting roads. Higher patrol frequency near borders and in remote areas. The universal military training means most travelers can defend themselves, but organized patrols deter bandits and respond to giant incursions or other threats. Patrol schedules are published so cooperatives can coordinate trade caravans with protection availability.

**Are there magical means of transportation?**

Almost none. Teleportation and similar magic are restricted under Pact as wasteful. Emergency use requires Tribunal justification just like other kingdoms. The state magical colleges could theoretically provide it but rarely do. The disciplined culture views magical shortcuts as weakness and laziness. Conventional travel by horse or foot is expected. Only critical military or diplomatic emergencies might justify magical transport, and even then it's controversial.

**How does magical transportation compare in speed, safety, and expense to non-magical means?**

When used, magical teleportation is instantaneous but requires hiring certified contractors, justifying to Tribunal, and paying enormous costs. Arranging it takes longer than conventional travel in most cases. Safety is high when performed by experts but accidents possible. Conventional travel by horse on quality roads is reliable, predictable, safe with patrols, and essentially free beyond provisions. The disciplined military culture views the journey as part of duty rather than obstacle. For 99.9% of situations, conventional travel is superior practically and culturally.

**Is there a public or private postal system, or does everyone of importance have to send messengers?**

Public postal system run by postal cooperatives coordinated nationally. Post stations exist in every garrison city and major settlements. Standardized rates for all citizens based on distance and urgency. Military dispatches receive priority handling through same system. No private couriers. Cooperatives compete for efficiency and reliability rather than profit, creating effective service. Important individuals use the same system as everyone else, reflecting egalitarian values. Letters and small packages handled routinely.

**Are there magical means of communication? How common and reliable are they? How expensive?**

**Sending Spell:** Available from state magical college practitioners. Limited to 25 words. Expensive (requires hiring certified caster and justifying under Pact). Reliable when properly cast.

**Usage:** Reserved for critical military communications, diplomatic emergencies, or situations where delay causes severe harm. Perhaps a few dozen sendings per year kingdom-wide.

**Cost:** Equivalent to month's wages for average cooperative worker. Prohibitively expensive for personal use.

**Reliability:** High success rate with trained casters. Recipient must be locatable.

**Frequency:** Rare. The disciplined culture views magical communication as unnecessary luxury when conventional postal system works adequately.

**Scrying:** Heavily restricted under Pact. Requires Tribunal authorization except for specific security purposes. State magical colleges can provide it but only for critical intelligence or emergency situations.

**Practical Reality:** Vanguard Reach functions as if magical communication doesn't exist for most purposes. The postal cooperative system handles everything efficiently enough that magic isn't needed.

**How are books produced?**

Hand-copied by scribes in cooperative scriptoriums. Professional scribes work in major cities, particularly Ithilvaeth. State magical colleges maintain copying operations for magical texts. Some clerical orders copy religious texts. Military manuals and training materials copied in quantity through coordinated scribal cooperatives. Each book individually transcribed taking weeks to months depending on length and complexity.

**Materials:** Parchment or vellum for important works, paper (imported) for common documents. Ink produced locally. Bindings created by leather-working cooperatives.

**Socialist Impact:** Scribal work organized cooperatively with production quotas and quality standards. Important texts (military manuals, legal codes, technical guides) receive priority over luxury items.

**Are books common or are they valuable hand-written objects?**

Valuable hand-written objects, but more accessible than in other kingdoms due to socialist distribution.

**Cost:** Single book costs several months' wages, but cooperative libraries and state institutions provide access. Military manuals distributed to officers at state expense.

**Ownership:**

- **Common citizens:** Few own personal books. Access through cooperative or public libraries.
- **Cooperative leaders:** May own 5-10 technical manuals and references.
- **Officers:** Receive military texts as part of position, don't personally own them.
- **State magical colleges:** Substantial collections for training and reference.
- **Public libraries:** Exist in major cities with cooperative funding, unusual feature.

**Common Books:** Military manuals, technical forge guides, legal codes, agricultural references, medical texts.

**Literacy:** High due to universal military training requiring basic reading. Perhaps 40-50% literacy in cities, 20-30% in rural areas. Military service teaches enough reading for orders and reports.

**Cultural Attitude:** Books are valuable but collectively owned. Public access through libraries reflects socialist values. Personal book collections seen as less important than shared resources.

**Is there freedom of the press? If not, who controls or censors it, by what means, and for what purpose?**

Limited freedom with state oversight.

**Legal Restrictions:**

- Content undermining military readiness or cooperative system prohibited
- Encouraging desertion or draft resistance illegal
- Advocating private ownership of major industries forbidden
- Treasonous material or enemy propaganda banned
- Heresy against established religious practices restricted
- Magical texts teaching forbidden spells illegal under Pact

**Control Mechanisms:**

- Scribal cooperatives self-regulate, refusing to copy prohibited material
- State reviews publications before mass copying authorized
- Military censors review anything related to defense or tactics
- Parliament can ban specific works by vote

**Enforcement:**

- Authors of banned material face charges
- Cooperatives copying illegal texts prosecuted
- Confiscation of prohibited books

**Practical Reality:** The slow pace of hand-copying means dangerous ideas spread too slowly to require aggressive censorship. The cooperative structure creates social pressure toward conformity. Most censorship focuses on military security rather than political thought. Recent years show growing tension as Economic Reformers push boundaries on acceptable criticism of socialist system. The brewing discontent hasn't yet triggered crackdowns but authorities watch carefully.

**Purpose:** Maintaining military readiness, preserving socialist system, preventing sedition, protecting state secrets, enforcing Pact compliance.
