_Title_: The Radiant Bloom  
_Domains_: Heat, Abundance  
_Creators_: Almariel and Pyrael  
_Pantheon_: Alorama

![[Pasted image 20250417094729.png]]

**Description**  
**Solana**, the God of Summer, is the embodiment of warmth, growth, and the abundance that comes with the season of life and prosperity. Created by [[Almariel, Lady of Light]]’s light and [[Pyrael, Aevari of Fire]]’s flame, Solana is the radiant deity who brings forth the bounty of the earth, ensuring that the fields are fertile, the fruit is ripe, and the warmth of the sun nurtures all living things. Known as **The Radiant Bloom**, Solana is adored for the life-giving energy she brings, as well as for the vibrant beauty that summer provides.

Solana is depicted as an exuberant figure whose presence exudes vitality and warmth. She wears robes of gold and green that seem to shimmer like sunlit fields. Her hair flows like rays of sunlight, and her eyes are bright with the fire of the midsummer sun. In her hands, she holds a staff adorned with blooming flowers, representing the abundance of life and the beauty of growth.

---

**Followers**  
Solana’s followers, including farmers, orchard keepers, and those whose livelihoods depend on the prosperity of the land, celebrate the peak of life’s abundance, nurturing growth in their fields and communities, and sharing the bounty they receive. Temples dedicated to Solana are often set amidst gardens or farmlands, filled with vibrant colors and the scent of summer blooms, providing a place where people come to give thanks for their blessings.

Solana’s teachings emphasize the joy of abundance and the importance of nurturing life. Her followers believe in sharing their prosperity, ensuring that everyone in the community enjoys the fruits of the summer harvest.

---

**Rituals and Offerings**

1. **The Midsummer Revelry**: Held during the summer solstice, this grand celebration includes feasts, music, and dancing. During this festival, the community gathers to honor Solana’s gifts, decorating her altars with flowers and lighting bonfires to symbolize the sun’s strength. The **Midsummer Revelry** is a joyous time when people share food, drink, and the warmth of fellowship.
    
2. **The Blessing of the Fields**: At the beginning of each growing season, the Sunwardens bless the fields by sprinkling them with water warmed by the sun and offering prayers to Solana. This ritual is believed to ensure a plentiful harvest and protect the crops from harm.
    
3. **Offerings**: Offerings to Solana include bundles of summer flowers, ripe fruit, and woven garlands. These are placed at her altars as tokens of gratitude for the warmth and abundance she provides.
    

---

**Sigils and Symbols**  
Solana’s symbol is a **sunburst surrounded by blooming flowers**, representing both the heat of the sun and the abundance it brings. Her followers wear this sigil as a charm or paint it on their barns and homes to invite her blessing. It is also used to decorate the fields and gardens during the **Midsummer Revelry** as a symbol of the joy and beauty of summer.

**Additional Details**  
**The Sunblessed**, those particularly favored by Solana, are said to possess a natural affinity for nurturing plants and bringing warmth wherever they go. Fields tended by the Sunblessed are always particularly lush, and their presence brings a sense of vitality and optimism to those around them. These individuals often lead the celebrations during the **Midsummer Revelry** and are called upon to bless new crops or gardens.

Solana’s influence is felt in every warm breeze, every field in bloom, and every moment spent in the joy of the sun’s warmth. She teaches that abundance is a gift to be shared, that the earth’s bounty is meant to be enjoyed by all, and that summer is a time to celebrate life in all its vibrant beauty.
