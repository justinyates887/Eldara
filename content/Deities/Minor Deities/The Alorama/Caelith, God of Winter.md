**Appearance**: Caelith is depicted as a tall, stoic figure with an ethereal, icy presence, draped in robes that seem woven from snow and frost. His eyes glow a pale, glacial blue, reflecting the harsh, unyielding beauty of winter. His crown is adorned with ice crystals and frosted evergreen branches.

**Followers**: Caelith’s devotees, often found in the northern, frost-covered reaches, are known as the "[[Frostwardens]]." They honor his teachings of resilience, patience, and preservation. Among his followers are human communities who endure harsh winters, as well as hermits and [[Druids]] who value solitude and the quiet strength of winter.

**Rituals**: The Frostwardens celebrate "The First Frost," marking the onset of winter with offerings of dried herbs, evergreen boughs, and preserved berries. During winter solstice, followers observe a day of silence, reflecting on endurance and inner strength, a time they call the "Frostfall Vigil."

**Sigils**: Caelith’s symbol is a stylized snowflake with an evergreen wreath encircling it, representing both the cold and the endurance of life through winter.

**Offerings**: Offerings to Caelith include preserved foods, evergreen sprigs, and silver or pale blue candles, symbolizing the cold, yet life-sustaining light of winter.
