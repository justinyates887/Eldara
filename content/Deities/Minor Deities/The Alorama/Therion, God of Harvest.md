_Title_: The Bountiful Guardian  
_Domains_: Agriculture  
_Creators_: Almariel and Feron  
_Pantheon_: Alorama

![[Pasted image 20250417100417.png]]

**Description**  
**Therion**, the God of Harvest, embodies the abundance of the earth and the fruition of hard work and nurturing care. Created by [[Almariel, Lady of Light]]’s wisdom and nurturing light and [[Feron, Aevari of the Earth]]’s mastery over the earth, Therion is revered as the deity who ensures the success of crops, the fertility of the soil, and the prosperity of those who work the land. Known as **The Bountiful Guardian**, Therion’s presence brings hope and gratitude to farmers, orchard keepers, and all who depend on the earth's bounty for sustenance.

Therion is depicted as a tall, broad figure dressed in robes of rich green and golden hues, symbolizing the ripe fields and the promise of harvest. His hair is adorned with sheaves of wheat and his eyes hold the warm glow of the sun at dusk. He carries a sickle and a cornucopia, representing the tools of harvest and the abundance he provides. Around him, vines and fruits are often shown flourishing, a sign of his influence over the natural world.

---

**Followers**  
Therion’s devoted followers, including farmers, gardeners, and those who work closely with the land. They maintain temples that are often built in the heart of farmlands or near orchards, serving as both places of worship and centers for community gatherings. The Harvestkeepers celebrate the cycles of planting, growth, and reaping, honoring Therion’s role in every step of the agricultural process.

Therion’s teachings emphasize hard work, patience, and respect for the land. His followers believe that by nurturing the earth, they honor Therion’s gifts and ensure the prosperity of their communities.

---

**Rituals and Offerings**

1. **The First Sowing**: At the beginning of each growing season, the **First Sowing** ritual is held to bless the seeds and ask Therion for a fruitful harvest. During this ceremony, seeds are anointed with blessed water and prayers are offered, asking for Therion’s guidance and favor.
    
2. **The Harvest Feast**: During the height of harvest, communities come together for the **Harvest Feast**. Tables are filled with the best produce of the season, and offerings are made at Therion’s altar, which is decorated with sheaves of wheat, fruit, and vegetables. The feast is a time of thanksgiving and celebration of the abundance provided by Therion.
    
3. **Offerings**: Offerings to Therion include bundles of wheat, ripe fruit, and the first cuttings of the harvest. These are placed at his altars as symbols of gratitude and to ensure continued prosperity for the following seasons.
    

---

**Sigils and Symbols**  
Therion’s symbol is a **sheaf of wheat crossed by a sickle**, representing both the labor of the harvest and the abundance it yields. His followers often wear this sigil as a charm, or carve it into barn doors and farming tools to invoke his blessing. It is also used to mark the entrance to fields, symbolizing Therion’s watchful presence over the crops.

**Additional Details**  
**The Earthbound**, those especially favored by Therion, are believed to possess an innate connection to the land. Their fields are known to flourish with exceptional vigor, and they can sense the needs of the soil and plants. The Earthbound are highly respected within agricultural communities, often leading the ceremonies of planting and harvest and advising farmers on how best to tend their crops.

Therion’s influence can be felt in every growing field, every ripe fruit, and every bountiful harvest. He teaches that the earth will always provide for those who treat it with care, patience, and respect, and that the cycle of sowing and reaping is a sacred dance that connects all life.
